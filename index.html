About Me

I'm a proud Guatemalan and an incoming M.S. student in Transportation Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley, where I completed my undergraduate degree in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research. I'm especially interested in research related to transportation and mobility systems, including optimizing routing problems, and I enjoy modeling and simulating matching and queueing problems that arise in real-world operational settings. Much of my work focuses on understanding how algorithms, data, and system structure interact to shape performance.

I currently work as a Data Fellow at Berkeley's Center for Educational Partnerships, where I support data analytics projects that inform educational access, outreach, and program evaluation across campus initiatives.

I'm a member of the student chapter of the Institute of Transportation Engineers (Cal ITE) and serve on the Operations Subteam for the Cal ASCE Transportation Competition Team, where I collaborate on projects related to transportation planning and operations.

My academic interests include vehicle routing, last-mile logistics, large-scale optimization, and machine learning applications, but I'm flexible and curious about applying these tools to other problem domains. I have a strong appreciation for computational organization and reproducibility, and I make a point to keep my work well-structured, clearly documented, and version controlled.

I also enjoy teaching and mentoring, and I believe that teaching is just as valuable for learning as it is for sharing knowledge. I like breaking down complex ideas into intuitive components and helping others build confidence with technical material.

Outside of academics, I'm an advocate for autism awareness, inspired by my younger brother. I also enjoy playing guitar and piano, emphasizing R&B, smooth jazz, and ballads; anything that is calm and relaxed. I'm a big fan of soccer, have supported Borussia Dortmund since 2017 (Heja BVB!), and enjoy playing the sport as well.
