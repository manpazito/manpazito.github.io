About Me

I’m a senior undergraduate in Industrial Engineering & Operations Research at the University of California, Berkeley. I’m especially interested in transportation systems, and I enjoy studying how matching and routing logistics can be optimized in real-world environments. Much of my work focuses on building models of operational systems and understanding how algorithms and data shape performance.

I currently work as a Data Fellow at Berkeley’s Center for Educational Partnerships, where I support data analytics projects that inform educational access, outreach, and program evaluation across campus initiatives.

I’m also conducting research on last-mile delivery optimization and capacitated vehicle routing problems (CVRP). This project is funded through the Firebaugh Research Scholars Program and examines structural properties of large routing instances, the performance of modern heuristics on spatial demand patterns, and scalable modeling approaches for practical last-mile logistics systems.
