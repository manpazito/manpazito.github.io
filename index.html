About Me

I’m a senior undergraduate in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at the University of California, Berkeley. I’m especially interested in transportation and mobility systems, and I enjoy modeling and simulating matching and queueing problems that arise in real-world operational settings. Much of my work focuses on understanding how algorithms, data, and system structure interact to shape performance.

I currently work as a Data Fellow at Berkeley’s Center for Educational Partnerships, where I support data management projects that inform educational access, outreach, and program evaluation across campus initiatives.

My academic interests include vehicle routing, last-mile logistics, and large-scale optimization, but I’m flexible and curious about applying these tools to other problem domains. I have a strong appreciation for computational organization and reproducibility, and I make a point to keep my work well-structured, clearly documented, and version controlled.

I also enjoy teaching and mentoring, and I believe that teaching is just as valuable for learning as it is for sharing knowledge. I like breaking down complex ideas into intuitive components and helping others build confidence with technical material.

Outside of academics, I’m an advocate for autism awareness, inspired by my younger brother. I’m also a multi-instrumentalist and enjoy playing both guitar and piano. I’m a big fan of soccer, have supported Borussia Dortmund since 2017 (Heja BVB!), and enjoy playing the sport as well.
